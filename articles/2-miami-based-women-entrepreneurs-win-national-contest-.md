# 2 Miami-based women entrepreneurs win national contest

Two South Florida women business owners are on their way to
growing million-dollar enterprises — something just 1.8 percent of women
entrepreneurs nationwide have achieved — as a result of winning the national
[Make Mine a Million $ Business](http://www.makemineamillion.org/) competition in New York City.

Among the 30 winners nationwide: Alejandra Bigai, owner of
[Romanicos Chocolate](http://www.romanicoschocolate.com/), a gourmet chocolate shop and factory in Miami, and
Michelle Villalobos, owner of [MIVISTA Consulting](http://www.mivistaconsulting.com/), which creates skill-building
workshops, seminars and events for individuals and organizations.

The Make Mine a Million $ Business program, an initiative of
Count Me In for Women’s Economic Independence and American Express OPEN, is
designed to help women entrepreneurs strengthen their vision, build their
strategy and take their businesses to $1 million and more in revenue.

[![Ale-80x800](https://web.archive.org/web/20120917125523im_/http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2017c31dcf1a8970b-120wi "Ale-80x800")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2017c31dcf1a8970b-pi)For Bigai, being a successful entrepreneur is a mixture of good
ideas, passion, drive and tools to succeed. Bigai knows a bit about recipes -- she started her business at 18th Avenue and Coral Way 11 years ago. “We make chocolate the way it is meant to be — healthy, beautiful and
delicious," she says.

To her, tools mean education, and as a Make Mine a Million $
Business program awardee, she and Villalobos received six-months of free
coaching and business education in Count Me In’s Business Accelerator Program,
among other prizes. “When you are an entrepreneur it’s in your genes to push
and push but if you don’t have the right tools maybe you are pushing in the
wrong direction,” says Bigai.

[![Villalobos960_px_length_headshot_fo-3](https://web.archive.org/web/20120917125523im_/http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2017c31dcf218970b-120wi "Villalobos960_px_length_headshot_fo-3")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2017c31dcf218970b-pi)Villalobos, who founded the [Women’s Success Summit](http://www.womenssuccesssummit.com/), said the
accelerator program includes intensive weekly coaching sessions, homework and
goal-setting. “So many of us get caught in this situation where we create
something and we are the ones running around doing everything. The idea is this
will teach us how to outsource, delegate, automate, systemize and scale it up.”

Villalobos and Bigai first competed with their two-minute
elevator pitches. After becoming finalists for their pitches, they then had to
submit a rigorous set of financials and goals, and were selected as winners.

Both Villalobos and Bigai say it was inspiring to
see past winners and how well they’ve done. “Some women have grown their
businesses to multimillions, and that was really powerful,” Villalobos says.

More information about the Make Mine a Million $ Business:
[www.makemineamillion.org](http://www.makemineamillion.org/).
