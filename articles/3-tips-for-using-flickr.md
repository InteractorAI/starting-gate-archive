# 3 tips (plus more) for using Flickr in your small business

[![TashaCunningham_biz](../images/6a00d83451b26169e201676288c176970b-120wi.jpg "TashaCunningham_biz")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e201676288c176970b-pi)My friend Tasha Cunningham, a contributing columnist in the Miami Herald and pricipal of [The Cunningham Group](http://www.thecunninghamgroup.com/), offers the lowdown on using the popular photo-sharing website, [Flickr](http://www.flickr.com/), with its 51 million users.

For a small business, Flickr offers another excellent avenue to market to the masses, Tasha says. She offers these tips on using Flickr:

1. Integrate your Flickr photo stream into your current website: This gives potential customers something interesting and engaging to look at when they log on to your site. It could potentially keep them browsing on your site longer.  
2. Share your Flickr activity on Facebook and Twitter: This is a great way to enhance your company level of engagement on social media networks, providing great visibility for you. Your photo could even go viral, creating more buzz for your brand.  
3. Allow others to download and share your Flickr photos: To help drive your company branding and marketing efforts, use Flickr to create an online media kit that others, including reporters and bloggers, can download with ease. Make you include high-resolution images of your company, logo, principals, products and services. Allowing others to download your photos on Flickr is a good way to leverage your exposure to potential customers.
