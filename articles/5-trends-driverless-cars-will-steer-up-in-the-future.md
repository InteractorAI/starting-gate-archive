# 5 trends driverless cars will steer up in the future

**By Joe Levy**

[![Levyuntitled](https://web.archive.org/web/20130819074622im_/http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2019104ba6b82970c-120wi "Levyuntitled")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2019104ba6b82970c-pi)

The wheels on the driverless cars go
round and round, round and round, round and round, the wheels on the cars go
round and round all by themselves…

Yes!  No more science fiction
fantasies. Driverless cars are around the corner, and it’s a reality.
 Just close your eyes and imagine: A city with self-driving cars dropping
off people and picking them up. No more looking for parking spaces, getting
traffic tickets, or need for multiple cars per household.

Automakers and tech companies are
developing complex technology that will allow cars to drive themselves;
possibly by the end of the decade, around the year 2018. [Fortune](http://features.blogs.fortune.cnn.com/2013/01/10/automakers-are-worried-about-driverless-cars/?section=magazines_fortune&utm_source=feedburner&utm_medium=feed&utm_campaign=Feed%3A+rss%2Fmagazines_fortune+%28Fortune+Magazine%29) reports that Google is leading the race by having
driverless cars already in the testing stages, followed by Audi, Toyota, and
Mercedes-Benz.  The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers
(IEEE) predicts that driverless cars will account for up to 75 percent of
vehicles on the road by the year 2040. Are you counting how old you’ll be?
Don’t worry; most of us will be here to witness it.

identify future market trends we can expect with the arrival of driverless
cars. **Here are five ‘No Mores’ clearCi’s crystal ball found when looking
into the future with driverless cars:**

   **No More Drivers**

Driverless cars mean no more
drivers; that’s obvious!

But is this something to be
particularly thrilled about? Well, it depends. If you experience constant road
rage, is time to celebrate! However, if you drive for a living or own a
driving-related business, you may want to start considering a new career or
business venture.

But don’t fret; when one road
closes, another one opens. In this case, the self-driving car industry will
benefit car-sharing services like Zipcar, public transportation, taxi services,
and rental car companies.
