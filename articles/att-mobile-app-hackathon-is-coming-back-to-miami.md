# AT&T Mobile App Hackathon is coming back to Miami

[![Hackathon](../images/6a00d83451b26169e2017c34725f99970b-800wi.jpg "Hackathon")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2017c34725f99970b-pi)  
*Opening night at the AT&T Mobile App Hackathon at the Univerity of Miami Life Science & Technology Park in August. #Backinhack is coming back. The photo is by The Miami Herald's Al Diaz.*

[The AT&T Mobile App Hackathon](http://mobileappmia2.eventbrite.com/#m_1_100) that broke all [local attendance records](the-hackathon-winners-are-and-why-south-florida-wins-too.md) last summer is back, says Milana Kuznetsova, of [ESENEM](http://www.esenem.com/), the local tech startup that is once again AT&T’s local partner for the contest. The hackathon will be Dec. 14-15.

Kuznetsova says the focus this time will be on user experience, with speakers including Mike Cuesta from [CareCloud](http://www.carecloud.com/) sharing their wisdom. Microsoft and Blackberry will be participating in the event, too. “Graphic designers are strongly encouraged to attend,” Kuznetsova says.

Another highlight: More than $10K in cash and prizes will be offered.

Like the last hackathon, the venue will be the [University of Miami Life Science & Technology Park](http://umlstp.com/) at 1951 NW 7th Ave. [RightSpace2Meet](http://www.rightspace2meet.com/) donated its new, modern co-working space inside to use for the free event. The Hackathon will be shorter this time -- starting Friday night at 6 p.m. and ending Saturday at 10 p.m. -- and the hackathon folks will supply plenty of free food and Red Bull to keep the hackers pumped up.

“We are bringing Sphero back since everyone had a ball with them at the last hackathon -- pun intended,” Kuznetsova says.

More than 100 people have already signed up. For more information and to register, go here: <http://mobileappmia2.eventbrite.com/#m_1_100>
