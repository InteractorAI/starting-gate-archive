# Büro Miami opening second location in South Beach

[![BuromiamigI_85277_render1](../images/6a00d83451b26169e2017c33cef6ae970b-320wi.jpg "BuromiamigI_85277_render1")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2017c33cef6ae970b-pi)Continuing to build on the [co-working craze](co-working-wave-with-entrepreneurial-twist-sweeps-into-south-florida.md) that is sweeping South Florida, [Büro Miami](http://buromiami.com/) plans to open its second "Urban Workspaces" location in January, says Büro founder and CEO Michael Feinstein. It will be a 10,000-square-foot waterfront property occupying the 2nd floor at 1815 Purdy Ave. in the Sunset Harbour neighborhood of South Beach.

Like in Midtown Miami location, which opened in 2010 and houses more than 50 companies -- many of them startups -- Büro South Beach members will get stylish fully serviced communal spaces starting at $250 for the open spaces and available on a month-to-month basis; private offices are also available. Like at its Midtown location, Büro also offers in-house workshops and entrepreneurial events.

 “Sunset Harbour is a natural fit for us -– it’s a hip, up-and-coming neighborhood with easy access and tons of amenities,” Feinstein says, adding that Büro South Beach will be the first co-working space in the area.  Sunset Harbour is home to newcomers Jugo Fresh, Flywheel and Pubbelly joining local favorites such as Green Monkey, Sardinia and Fresh Market.
