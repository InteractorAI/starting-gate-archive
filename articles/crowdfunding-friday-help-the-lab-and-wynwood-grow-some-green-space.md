# Crowdfunding Friday: Help The LAB and Wynwood grow some green space

[![Rendering2](../images/6a00d83451b26169e2019b008ad90c970b-800wi.jpg "Rendering2")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2019b008ad90c970b-pi)

Wynwood has so
much going for it -– great art, a neighborly feel, creative eateries, lots of
startup energy, and did I say great art? But it sorely lacks butterflies, bees, parks and trees.

Yet, there's hope.

[The LAB Miami](http://www.thelabmiami.com/)
recently launched a [crowdfunding campaign](http://neighbor.ly/ideagarden) to turn its dreary alleyway  into an open green space dubbed the
IdeaGarden (rendering above). The Lab is using a platform for civic projects called Neighbor.ly  to raise $30,000. The LAB has raised about
$7,000 and has 21 days to go. The LAB hopes to unveil the garden by Art Basel.

The LAB’s pitch: By
leveraging the local creative community’s talent, we will build Wynwood’s first
green open space that also reflects the atmosphere of The LAB and neighborhood:
a congregation point for creativity, ideation and innovation. The garden will
be a site to  host classes or build
projects around sustainability and emerging
green technologies. It will be a destination for healthy living with farmers
markets, community gardens and yoga seminars. It will be a place to recharge
not only your laptop through its solar-powered charging stations, but also your
peace-of-mind as you relax under the shade of native vegetation.

 “This
is an initiative that will hopefully kickstart more green projects in Wynwood,
where there is an extreme lack of green space. The garden will be a place for
members and the public to work, attend events and workshops, host film
screenings, farmers markets, etc.,” said Wifredo Fernandez, co-founder of The
LAB.

The ultimate goal: replicating
this model throughout Wynwood (Yes, there is space -- how about some rooftop gardens?). But first there’s an IdeaGarden to fund, plant
and grow because Wynwood needs some butterflies, bees, parks and trees and we need to start somewhere.
