# Enter Knight Entrepreneurial Fellows contest for chance to win free tickets to SIME MIA

**By
Demian Bellumio**

[![Sime-logo-black-trans (1)](../images/6a00d83451b26169e2019b00b14442970c-320wi.jpg "Sime-logo-black-trans (1)")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2019b00b14442970c-pi)Ten startups
will win two golden tickets each to [SIME
MIA](http://www.simemia.co/), a highly
anticipated technology conference that will be held Dec. 3-4 at New World Center
in Miami Beach.

The winners, who
will be designated as Knight Entrepreneurial Fellows, will also receive a
one-on-one coaching session with an international investor. This is a great
opportunity for startups to deliver pitches and receive the mentoring they need
to reach the next step in their trajectory towards success.

SIME’s Ola
Ahlvarsson, MIA Collective and [The John
S. and James L. Knight Foundation](http://knightfoundation.org/) are creating a conference unique in its depth
of discussion and ability to keep all attendees on their feet.

SIME
MIA will not be a typical digital conference. The sounds of a live symphony and
people offering free hugs will greet attendees, who will receive a glass of
champagne and a strawberry as the event kicks off at 9 in the morning. Whether
one is a hugger or a fan of classical music, what stands out about this
conference is the top-notch list of speakers from Europe, the United States
and Latin America who will be visiting Miami,
many of them for the first time**.**

Panel
discussions will be led by industry leaders, such as Fon’s
[Martin Varsavsky](http://www.knightfoundation.org/blogs/knightblog/2013/10/22/martin-varsavsky-entrepreneurs-have-prepared-failure-success/) and Gigaom’s [Paul
Walborsky](https://twitter.com/PaulWalborsky), and aimed at
connecting the audience with the speakers. Knight Foundation Entrepreneurial
Fellows will have the opportunity to mingle with these industry leaders, while
learning about the latest trends that are at the forefront of the digital industry on a global scale. SIME
MIA will cover topics such as big data, venture capital and artificial intelligence, all of which are
rarely explored in other conferences in South Florida.

SIME MIA’s newly
added list of speakers include: [Caterina Fake](https://twitter.com/Caterina), CEO of [Findery](https://findery.com/), co-founder of Flickr and Hunch, and founding partner of [Founder Collective](http://foundercollective.com/); [Navin Thukkaram](https://twitter.com/dreamnavin), former founder and COO
of [Qwiki](http://www.qwiki.com/), which was sold to Yahoo! in July 2013; [Andy Kleinman](https://twitter.com/andykleinman), chief business officer at [Scopely](http://www.scopely.com/); [Francisco Alvarez](http://www.riverwoodcapital.com/#!/team/partners/francisco-alvarez-demalde), founder and partner of
[Riverwood Capital
Partners](http://www.riverwoodcapital.com/#!/home);
[Adriana Cisneros](http://www.adrianacisneros.com/), CEO and vice chairman of [Cisneros](http://www.cisneros.com/); and [Isaac Lee](https://twitter.com/isaacleep), CEO of [Fusion](http://fusion.net/) and president of news for [Univision](http://www.univision.com/).

To become a
[Knight Foundation Entrepreneurial Fellow](http://vimeo.com/78465014), create a video of 60 seconds or less
to convince the judges that you deserve to attend SIME MIA more than anyone
else. Upload the finished product to Instagram, Facebook and/or YouTube by
11:59pm on Nov. 20, 2013.

You must
mention/tag @simemiami and @knightfdn and share it as much as possible on all
social media platforms using  #SIMEMIA.
The 10 winners will receive two tickets to SIME MIA. Winners will be
announced on Nov. 27.

*Demian Bellumio is founding
partner of MIA Collective, COO of Senzari, a serial entrepreneur and an angel
investor.*

*Read Q&As with some of the speakers on the Knight Foundation blog: [Shel Israel](http://www.knightfoundation.org/blogs/knightblog/2013/9/23/shel-israel-awareness-essential-weighing-benefits-tech-against-loss-privacy/), [Christian Hernandez](http://www.knightfoundation.org/blogs/knightblog/2013/10/8/christian-hernandez-miami-needs-high-profile-companies-capture-investor-interest/) and [Martin Varsavsky](http://www.knightfoundation.org/blogs/knightblog/2013/10/22/martin-varsavsky-entrepreneurs-have-prepared-failure-success/).*
