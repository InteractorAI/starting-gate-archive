# Entrepreneurship Datebook: What's coming up after Thanksgiving

**![Techegg](https://web.archive.org/web/20160123193959im_/http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e201b7c7f0e64c970b-120wi "Techegg")SIME MIA:**The conference that has explored technology trends by bringing in speakers from Singularity University, Hollywood and the Oslo Freedom Forum is returning to Miami Beach for a third year Dec. 1-2 . Speakers on Dec. 1 include startup founders, artists, musicians, filmmakers, a neuroscientist and a Special Olympics world champion. Speakers recently added: NBA star Luol Deng, Christine Tsai of 500 Startups; Val Spiridonov, full body transplant candidate; and Erik Voorhees, creator of the instant Bitcoin. On Dec. 2, there will be half-day summits on fintech and big data. Tickets and info: [simemia.co](http://www.simemia.co/) (get 50% off with discount code  **SimeMIA50)**

**ITPALOOZA:** Now in its fourth year, the conference on Dec. 3 at Nova Southeastern University in Davie brings together the South Florida IT community. The all-day event includes discussions, an expo, a hiring fair and more. Bring two unwrapped toys for charity for free admission. [Tickets and info: www.itpalooza.com.](http://www.eventbrite.com/e/itpalooza-2015-tickets-17567561048?aff=itpweb)

**AT&T HACKATHON:** ITPalooza and AT&T are teaming up for a free hackathon at Nova Southeastern University on Dec. 4-5; form teams, build solutions in a weekend and compete for prizes. More info: [http://developer.att .com/community/events](http://developer.att.com/community/events).

**TECHWEEK MIAMI:** Activities Dec. 7-13 will include the national finals of the Launch startup competition Dec. 10 with startup champions from Miami and five other cities. Another featured event is [Hatch Match Miami](http://thelabmiami.com/event/hatch-match-miami-2015/) on Dec. 8. Tickets and info:[techweek.com/miami](http://www.techweek.com/miami).

### STARTING GATE

[South Florida startups Kairos and Genetics Networks](kairos-funded-by-florida-institute-500-startups.md)received funding from the Florida Institute for the Commercialization of Public Resources. [Büro Group opened a co-working center in Coconut Grove](b%C3%BCro-group-opens-coconut-grove-co-working-center-its-fourth-location.md), its fourth location. [CappSci Inventors announced the winners](cappsci-inventors-program-awards-inaugural-innovation-prizes.md)of its first two $100,000 prizes. The winners will build their solutions at the Patricia and Phillip Frost Museum of Science. Read more on the [Starting Gate](index.md) blog at MiamiHerald.com/business.
