# Forum bringing Americas' social entrepreneurship ventures to the Miami stage

[![Steve-Wozniak](../images/6a00d83451b26169e2017ee4fbc1a0970d-120wi.jpg "Steve-Wozniak")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2017ee4fbc1a0970d-pi)
[![Jessica_jackley](../images/6a00d83451b26169e2017c3357ef59970b-120wi.jpg "Jessica_jackley")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2017c3357ef59970b-pi)What do Kofi Annan and Steve Wozniak have in common? They are both speaking at The [Americas Business Council (abc\*) Foundation](http://cts.businesswire.com/ct/CT?id=smartlink&url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.abcfound.org%2F&esheet=50407024&lan=en-US&anchor=Americas+Business+Council+%28abc*%29+Foundation&index=1&md5=04858629f13b5789596adf936484c029)’s [2012 abc\* Continuity Forum](http://cts.businesswire.com/ct/CT?id=smartlink&url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.abccontinuityforum.org&esheet=50407024&lan=en-US&anchor=2012+abc*+Continuity+Forum&index=2&md5=e34700ad8bf88384695d3705f18ef545) this week at the Ritz-Carlton Coconut Grove. Other speakers include: Chris Hughes of New Republic and co-founder of Facebook, [Jessica Jackley](http://www.crunchbase.com/person/jessica-jackley "Jessica Jackley"), co-founder of Kiva.org;  Cesar Conde, Univision Networks president, Harvard economist Roland Fryer, explorer and filmmaker Alexandra Cousteau, hip-hop recording artist Emmanuel Jal and others. (Jackley and Wozniak are pctured above.)

The three-day forum Nov. 12-14 -- now in its second year -- provides a platform for social entrepreneurs from the Americas to share their organizations’ work. The [Americas Business Council](http://www.abcfound.org/), a nonprofit that calls itself a think-do tank to support high-impact leaders and social entrepreneurial projects in the Americas, is partnering with [Ashoka](http://cts.businesswire.com/ct/CT?id=smartlink&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.ashoka.org%2F&esheet=50407024&lan=en-US&anchor=Ashoka&index=3&md5=a3fb273f4d07861d60c297918c08bbfe), the world’s largest association of social entrepreneurs, and [Innovadores de America](http://cts.businesswire.com/ct/CT?id=smartlink&url=http%3A%2F%2Finnovadoresdeamerica.org&esheet=50407024&lan=en-US&anchor=Innovadores+de+America&index=4&md5=4122347886cf16ee9d34f8650b4babb0), an organization that awards individuals for superior social, scientific, business and cultural achievements in Latin America. Out of 117 pre-screened initiatives by these two organizations, the abc\* Foundation [selected 33 to present](http://www.abccontinuityforum.com/program/entrepreneurial) at the 2012 Continuity Forum. These include a company that transforms urban building surfaces into green landscapes, a project to develop earthquake resistant buildings in developing countries and create jobs at the same time, a system that allows members  to enlist help from one another as a form of currency; and another one bringing education to underserved Mexican communities via technology, to name a few. At the end of the Forum, the abc\* Foundation will select three projects to receive funding and hands-on advisory, as well as media and marketing support for two years.

To read about the social entrepreneurs, plus for event details and ticket information, go to [http://www.abccontinuityforum.org](http://cts.businesswire.com/ct/CT?id=smartlink&url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.abccontinuityforum.org&esheet=50407024&lan=en-US&anchor=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.abccontinuityforum.org&index=5&md5=55985a62cf3ed18135d88415d71abae9).
