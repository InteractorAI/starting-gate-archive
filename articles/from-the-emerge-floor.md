# From the eMerge floor

Between booth displays and start-up tables, the eMerge floor is a trove of ideas – some so straightforward you can’t figure out why they are just now coming to fruition. Among them:

* Dylan Goubin of four-year-old Wikipixel, based in Aventura, has created a new app, Citizenshield, that enables residents of cities, universities and condos to report problems such as potholes and grafitti in real time; others can also see that the problems have been reported and resolved. He hopes to market the customized apps to local municipalities. [![Citizenshield](../images/6a00d83451b26169e201bb08295c92970d-320wi.jpg "Citizenshield")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e201bb08295c92970d-pi)
* Pim de Witte, 20, originally from Holland and now in Miami, has already created successful tech companies. His latest not-for-profit technology has been used to track Ebola patients in West Africa. The latest effort from his firm, White Spell, is a service that links consumers with fitness content for at-home workouts.
