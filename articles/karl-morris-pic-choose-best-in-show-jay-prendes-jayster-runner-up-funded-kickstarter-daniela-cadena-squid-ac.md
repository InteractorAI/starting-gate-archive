# Miami's Got Tech! Yes, indeed

[![Photo](../images/6a00d83451b26169e201a73d73af1d970d-800wi.jpg "Photo")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e201a73d73af1d970d-pi)

Miami's Got Tech!, the third annual [Greater Miami Chamber of Commerce](http://www.miamichamber.com/) event that highlights emerging entrepreneurs and collegiate innovation, attracted a record crowd to Venture Hive in downtown Miami Thursday night. Along with networking, eight teams exhibited their technology products or projects at the event, and judges evaluated them.

[

![Photo (11)](https://web.archive.org/web/20150630010558im_/http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e201a511688a5a970c-250wi "Photo (11)

")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e201a511688a5a970c-pi)The winners were:

**Best in Show: Karl Morris, an FIU student, and Xabriel J. Collazo Mojica of [Colada Studios](http://www.coladastudios.com/), which launches apps, for the Pic & Choose app.**

**Runner up: Jay Prendes, an FIU alum, and Lea Mcnaughton for [Jayster](http://www.findjayster.com/), the Bluetooth-smart keychain so you will never lose your keys again.**
