# Crowdfunding Friday: Miami startup offers shoes with a story -- and social entrepreneurial twist

[![Juntos 2130](https://web.archive.org/web/20130821035036im_/http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e20192ac93d7ba970d-800wi "Juntos 2130")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e20192ac93d7ba970d-pi)

 Like the
flip-flop is to Rio, this simple canvas shoe is to cities like Guayaquil, Ecuador.

 As an unofficial symbol of street fashion, El Zapato de Lona, is a classic canvas lace-up shoe that has
become  a working class badge of honor in
that part of the world, says Mauricio Laniado, co-founder of [Juntos](http://www.juntosproject.com/), a Miami-based startup.

The shoes caught the
eye of the first-generation Ecuadorian during his recent travels and inspired him to start
his first company to offer his
own version of these iconic shoes -– with a hip twist.

Available in  black canvas with white piping, white and powder blue, the shoes also features a a map of Ecuador inside -- so that when you wear them, you never
forget where they came from, he says. They are set to retail for $48, and
Laniado already has a U.S.
production partner lined up.

This story has a
social entrepreneurial twist too: Each pair of shoes purchased lets you do good,
as an education backpack filled with a year’s worth of crucial school supplieswill be donated to an Ecuadorian at-risk
youth in the same neighborhoods where the style hails from. That’s one
education backpack for every pair of shoes bought, says Laniado**.**

Laniado -– who co-founded
the company with Andrew Tupper -– says he plans to create more products inspired
by iconic styles  from around the world
that bring on warm feelings of home. But this is Juntos’ first product and  the company is introducing it on crowdfunding
platform [Indiegogo](http://www.indiegogo.com/projects/the-juntos-shoe-look-good-do-good?show_todos=true). In just over a week, the company
has already surpassed its goal of $!0,000 but now has set stretch goals. Its
campaign ends Sept. 4.

To view and take
part in Juntos’ Indiegogo campaign,  visit [FirstStepJuntos.com](http://firststepjuntos.com/).  A digital look book for the Zapato de
Lona is at [www.JuntosProject.com/lb](http://www.juntosproject.com/lb).
