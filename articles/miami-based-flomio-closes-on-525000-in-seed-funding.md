# Miami-based Flomio closes on $525,000 in seed funding

I am admittedly late on posting this news, but Miami-based [Flomio](http://flomio.com/), maker of a platform for building NFC and RFID enabled applications, [closed a seed round](http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1554837/000155483712000001/xslFormDX01/primary_doc.xml) of $525,000, led by RMR Capital, and included participation from TechStars, the Cloud Power Fund, Matthew Lally ([Augme](http://augme.com/), [Pickflair](http://pickflair.com/)), and [Clint Watson](http://clintavo.com/) ([FASO](http://faso.com/)), [TechCrunch reported](http://techcrunch.com/2012/07/27/nfc-still-has-legs-flomio-closes-on-half-a-million-in-seed-funding-for-nfc-based-products-services/) on Friday.

Flomio, founded by Richard Grundy,  was recently accepted into [TechStars Cloud](http://www.techstars.com/cloud/), the cloud computing-focused incubator. Read more about its technology [here](http://techcrunch.com/2012/07/27/nfc-still-has-legs-flomio-closes-on-half-a-million-in-seed-funding-for-nfc-based-products-services/).

So last week was a good week for South Florida tech startups getting funding. What will this month bring?
