# National Innovative App Challenge calls for entries from middle and high school students

The [Verizon Foundation](http://www.verizonfoundation.org/), in
partnership with the [Technology Student Association](http://www.tsaweb.org/),
have launched the 2013-2014 [Innovative App Challenge](http://www.verizonfoundation.org/appchallenge), giving
middle and high school students in South Florida and across the country a
chance to develop a concept for a mobile app and bring it to market. Prizes
include Samsung tablets, cash grants of $20,000 for the winners’ schools and
the opportunity to team up with app development experts from the [Massachusetts
Institute of Technology Media Lab](http://www.media.mit.edu/) to build and bring their apps to
life. Verizon will help winning teams bring their app to the [Google Play](https://play.google.com/store?hl=en) store. Submissions
will be accepted through Dec. 3. More information, contest rules and
application procedures: [www.verizonfoundation.org/appchallenge](http://www.verizonfoundation.org/appchallenge).
