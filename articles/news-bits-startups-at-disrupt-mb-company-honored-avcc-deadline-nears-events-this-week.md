# News bits: Startups at Disrupt, MB company honored, AVCC deadline nears, events this week

Who says September is a slow month in South Florida? Maybe for tourism but not for tech! A few news highlights:

At least two South Florida tech companies are at TechCrunch Disrupt Startup Alley this week in San Francisco. Ninut, a Miami-based startup that has developed the [Paymesh](https://www.paymesh.com/) digital cash app, will be exhibiting Tuesday. Read a Startropica interview [here](http://startropica.com/paymesh-disrupting-the-disrupters/).  [Geopon](http://www.geopon.com/), a mobile commerce solution for geo-targeted coupons and more, exhibited at Startup Alley Monday. Geopon, which won the [Business Plan Challenge FIU Track](http://www.miamiherald.com/2012/05/06/2784079/app-targets-exploding-mobile-coupon.html) this year, also had its soft launch last week.

[Nova Point of Sale](http://www.empact100.com/company/1009-nova-point-of-sale), a Miami Beach software company offering point of sale software for mall carts,  
kiosks, and other retailers worldwid., made the [Empact 100](http://www.empact100.com/100) list of companies. Through [Empact](http://www.iempact.com/), the Startup America Partnership, and a number of other partners, the Empact Showcase is a compilation of the top entrepreneurs in the US age 30 and under with revenues over $100,000.

This is the last week to apply to present at Florida International University's [Americas Venture Capital Conference](http://avcc.fiu.edu/) (AVCC). The deadline to apply is Sept 14. Find out more [here](http://www.avcc.fiu.edu/presenting_companies.html).

 The following is from [Startup Digest Miami](http://adk.tumblr.com/post/31269833634/miami-startup-digest-09-10-2012) by Andrej Kostresevic: [SFTA has a very interesting event coming up](http://www.southfloridatech.org/upcoming-events/upcoming-sfta-september-25-event-sponsored-by-citrix/) on 9/25, aiming to celebrate the anniversary of the first smartphone ([apparently developed in SFL in the late 80s/early 90s](http://startupdigest.us1.list-manage.com/track/click?u=92be899ef5a892c60b4a6cd97&id=e23a0bd688&e=60808fd614)), by bringing together high-visibility names including John Sculley, former Apple CEO, and Scott Moody, founder of AuthenTec, the Florida company Apple just  
acquired for $367 Million. SFTA is also looking for [3 cool mobile startups](http://startupdigest.us1.list-manage.com/track/click?u=92be899ef5a892c60b4a6cd97&id=a49d3c154a&e=60808fd614) to present at the event.  
  
Also, according to last week's StartupDigest, this week also brings [Bootstrap week](http://startupdigest.us1.list-manage1.com/track/click?u=92be899ef5a892c60b4a6cd97&id=3cdbb9b705&e=60808fd614), filled with free webinars on mobile app development.
