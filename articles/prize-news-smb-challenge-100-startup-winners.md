# Prize news: Small Business Challenge, $100 Startup winners

[![128_small](https://web.archive.org/web/20121129050524im_/http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2017c33fe7084970b-200wi "128_small")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2017c33fe7084970b-pi)A Doral small business came in third place in the [Small Business Challenge](http://smbchallenge.com/), a nationwide contest that attracted more than 1,000 entries. The  local winner: [Little Heroes Learning Center](https://littleheroeslearning.com/), with 14 employees, is dedicated to providing a high-caliber preschool program in a "homelike" environment to children, parents and staff, says its director, Daisy Acosta. The company will use the prize money -- $10,000 -- to hire a curriculum assistant, Acosta said. The SMBChallenge was sponsored by Miami-based [EarlyShares](http://www.earlyshares.com/).

And the [$100 Startup Competition](http://www.typepad.com/site/blogs/6a00d83451b26169e20168e5f84454970c/post/6a00d83451b26169e2017c333da89d970b/edit), sponsored by Books & Books, the Coral Gables Chamber, FIU and LAB Miami, took place Saturday at Books & Books. Contestants were judged on their ideas for a business that could be launched for $100. And the winners were:

1st Place, $300 and Coral Gables Chamber Business Membership: Ilya Fonarov, Hospital Patient Advocate Referral Service  
2nd Place, $150: Edwin Rosario, P.E. After Hours - Structured PhysEd After School  
3rd Place, $100: Tom Ordonez, [Miami Code School for Kids](http://www.miamicodeschool.com/)  
Honorable Mention: Susan Krupnick-Gregorie, e-Portfolios for Performing Arts Students

Congratulations to all
