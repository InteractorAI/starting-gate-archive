# Save the dates: Join Great Miami Makeathon March 31-April 2

[MANO](http://manoamericas.org/) is announcing the Great Miami Makeathon, a three-day design and prototype challenge to propel Miami’s maker community, taking place on the weekend of March 31 through April 2, 2017.

Co-organized by [The Idea Center at Miami Dade College](http://theideacenter.co/) and [Moonlighter](http://moonlighter.co/), and with the generous support of the [John S. and James L. Knight Foundation](https://www.knightfoundation.org/) and [StartUp FIU](http://startup.fiu.edu/), the Great Miami Makeathon encourages participants to develop functional prototypes that address the real-world needs of three of Miami’s hottest new learning and recreational spaces: the [Miami Science Barge](http://www.miamisciencebarge.org/), [Wynwood Yard](http://www.thewynwoodyard.com/), and the [American Museum of the Cuban Diaspora](http://thecuban.org/). Selected designs and prototypes are expected to center around technology, sustainability and education. Winners will receive a cash prize, the opportunity to work with partner venues to turn their prototypes into reality, and prominent placement at the upcoming [Maker Faire Miami](http://miami.makerfaire.com/) on April 8 and 9, 2017 at Miami Dade College’s Wolfson Campus.

The Great Miami Makeathon is open to the public and its organizers encourage anyone interested in participating in the design and prototype challenge to pull together a team of no less than two and no more than five people and register via Eventbrite ([link here](http://bit.ly/MiamiMakeathon)).

Prior to the Great Miami Makeathon, organizers will host a series of free educational workshops on design thinking, 3D printing, laser cutting, woodworking, and electronics to equip challenge participants with skills and practices required for the design and prototype process.

“Great Miami Makeathon seeks to show how the creative power of our incredible maker community can be harnessed to help local businesses and nonprofits innovate. With this event, our partner venues, whom we affectionately refer to as “the Barge, the Yard and the Cuban,” will crowdsource solutions from local makers to enhance their spaces and create more engaging and innovative user experiences for their patrons while conserving resources,” said Ric Herrero, co-founder of MANO.

MANO is a Miami-based non-profit organization dedicated to fostering inclusive, problem-solving communities through educational programs and initiatives. For more information about Great Miami Makeathon, and to register for our launch event at The Idea Center at Miami Dade College on March 27, please visit [miamimakeathon.com](https://www.miamimakeathon.com/), and follow us on Twitter via [@MiamiMakeathon](https://twitter.com/miamimakeathon).
