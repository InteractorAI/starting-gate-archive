# SCORE Miami-Dade’s “Special Forces” program helps companies go to next level

![Score_SpecialForces_Logo_2Color (1)](../images/6a00d83451b26169e2019b00b08c06970d-120wi.jpg "Score_SpecialForces_Logo_2Color (1)")[SCORE
Miami-Dade](http://miamidade.score.org/), a non-profit organization dedicated to helping small
businesses grow, has created a new program called, “Special Forces,” to help companies reach
higher levels of performance, sales and profitability. [“Special Forces”](http://scoremiamisf.moonfruit.com/)  provides
businesses with a dedicated team of certified SCORE counselors who have
expertise in the specific challenges faced by the business.

“Whether a company needs help with
marketing, sales, operations, distribution, financing, or staff, SCORE Miami-Dade’s Special Forces has certified counselors with expertise in all of these areas,”
says Phil Harris, Special Forces director.

Already, businesses in a Special
Forces pilot program have seen excellent growth and improvement.

“I’m a small business person without
access to business consultants,” says Tania Garza, president of Advak Technologies in
Hialeah. “SCORE changed that with Special Forces, giving us access to a team of
experienced business professionals. Together, we analyzed my business and developed a plan to
target my greatest challenges and have since had improvement in sales, financial
reporting and operations.”

Special Forces first captures data
about the business from a company evaluation survey, personal interviews and
visits with the management team. Together, SCORE Special Forces consultants and the
management team determine the greatest business challenges. Other SCORE counselors provide focused advice to best help the
business with its specific needs.

“From financing to marketing, my mentors provided useful insights. Counting on them throughout my start-up phase was like having a seasoned partner in the business,” says Juan Brandt, who opened Sky Zone, Miami's first indoor trampoline park last October.

To be selected for the program, a
business needs to have a minimum of $1 million in sales and at least five employees.
When the business is pleased with the recommendations and results, SCORE
requests a small donation to help support Special Forces with efforts to expand to
other businesses.

For more information, or to schedule
a Special Forces onsite workshop series, contact Phil Harris at [pharris@scoremiami.org](https://web.archive.org/web/20131107143752/mailto:pharris@scoremiami.org). More information about SCORE Miami-Dade: [http://miamidade.score.org](http://miamidade.score.org/)
