# Social Media Club South Florida turns five years young, holding celebration event Tuesday

**By Christine de la Huerta**

[![Smc](https://web.archive.org/web/20131112093506im_/http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2019b00f590e3970d-120wi "Smc")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e2019b00f590e3970d-pi)

Time flies when you spend so much of it
online!  This fall, Social Media Club South Florida (SMCSF) turns five
years young.  From its humble beginnings, the founders simply wished to
create a space for like-minded people to connect.  Somehow, the club grew
into much more than that.  
  
Today,
SMCSF is one of the largest chapters in the country.

Social Media Club South Florida aims to raise
awareness and educate local professionals and students about the value of
emerging social media tools for marketing communications, public relations,
customer service, recruitment, knowledge sharing and more. The club promotes
social media as an essential communications tool, working alongside traditional
media to improve the exposure, efficiency, and prospects of all organizations.
This applies to commercial, not-for-profit, educational and governmental
institutions. To meet these growing needs, SMCSF holds monthly meetings
and disseminates information to engage participants in social media, including
presentations from social media enthusiasts, open discussions, and networking
opportunities.

One such opportunity
has arrived this week as SMCSF celebrates its 5-year anniversary.

On Nov. 12 at The LAB Miami, SMCSF is hosting a celebratory
meet-up: "Stop Faking It!"  The program will reveal a myriad
of  dos and don'ts in social media when it comes to authenticity. Why
should you choose real followers over fake ones? How can companies and
individuals alike truly create a an authentic online brand presence?  How
do genuine engagement and grounded grassroots initiatives gather speed to
benefit your bottom line? These topics and much more will be addressed while
the panel answers questions and invites audience participation.

The panel discussion will feature local notables from the social media club
circles, including:  
\* Craig Agranoff @lapp, Co-founder, Gripd.com  
\* Gary Bahadur, CEO, KRAA Security  
\* Maria de los Angeles @vicequeenmaria, Freelance Multimedia Storyteller  
\* Janie Sederberg @janiec, Executive Vice President, Brand Experience at ONE
Sotheby's International Realty  
\* Grant Stern @grantstern, Owner, Morningside Mortgage Corporation

Please visit the [event page on Facebook](https://www.facebook.com/groups/smcsf/permalink/10151946519650255/) for
more information.
