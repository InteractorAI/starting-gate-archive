# Tech Talk: Magic Leap, Alta5, upcoming deadlines & more

**![Newrony](../images/6a00d83451b26169e201b8d2822fcc970c-250wi.jpg "Newrony")By Nancy Dahlberg / ndahlberg@miamiherald.com**

The secretive Magic Leap may be fund-raising again, and with that may pull a $6 billion to $8 billion valuation out of its hat. That’s from sources of the tech blog BackChannel.

[According to BackChannel](https://backchannel.com/magic-leap-could-be-looking-at-an-8-billion-valuation-28eaa0294ad1), part of Wired Media Group, Plantation-based Magic Leap is raising a Series D funding round led by Chinese ecommerce giant Alibaba. It’s not sewn up yet though. That’s from sources of tech writer/editor Jessi Hempel, who also wrote [one of the first long features](https://www.wired.com/2016/04/went-inside-magic-leaps-mysterious-hq-heres-saw/) about the augmented reality tech company with very little about its actual technology (that NDA, you know) about a year ago in Wired.

If this funding news is true, the cash runway may have shortened up a bit. About 15 months ago, [Magic Leap raised $793.5 million](http://www.miamiherald.com/news/business/article57894443.html) led by Alibaba, on top of $592 million [previously raised](http://www.miamiherald.com/news/business/article3196864.html) from Google and other VCs, for a total of nearly $1.4 billion on a $4.5 billion valuation – and that’s for a company that had yet to reveal its technology to the world. At the time CEO Rony Abovitz said that money would take the company through commercial launch.

“This funding allows us to accelerate the move from product development to pilot manufacturing, manufacturing and commercial launch,” [Abovitz told me](http://www.miamiherald.com/news/business/article57894443.html) in a phone interview at the time. “But beyond that, this gives the company a very long-term runway – effectively we can be sustainable. We may not need to raise capital again – it doesn’t mean we won’t – but it gives us the ability to think well beyond the initial product launch.”

Magic Leap still hasn’t launched a product, nor announced a timeline, but the fund-raise could suggest a launch is imminent – or not. In what may be another sign: Abovitz called on developers and artists to sign up to work on new content for the platform, via Twitter. Abovitz has said Magic Leap will launch its head gear when it’s ready, and with mixed reality content to wow the world.

In other Magic Leap news, as reported [earlier this week](http://www.miamiherald.com/news/business/article149583309.html), the mixed reality company has settled itse gender discrimination lawsuit, claiming a boys club culture among other things, for undisclosed terms.

Whether or not the big ML raise materializes in 2017, it will be a good year for South Florida funding. Boca Raton-based Modernizing Medicine saw to that this week, [raising $231 million](http://www.miamiherald.com/news/business/technology/article149663179.html) in funding from private equity firm Warburg Pincus and bringing its total funding to some $330 million.

![Rocco](https://web.archive.org/web/20181001181525im_/http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e201b8d2822fe4970c-800wi "Rocco")

In other Miami tech news, [Alta5](http://www.miamiherald.com/news/business/technology/article138984328.html) vaulted into the spotlight at its 500 Startups Demo Day in San Francisco on Thursday night, scoring favorable press out of the gate for the Miami-based fintech startup, which created an automation platform for individual investors. The live-streamed event attracted more than 500 investors, said Alta5 co-founder Rocco Savage (pictured above, pitching at Demo Day). [TechCrunch called Alta5](https://techcrunch.com/gallery/our-favorite-startups-from-500-startups-20th-class/slide/2/) one of the top eight in the batch of 41 500 Startup companies. Mattermark ranked the batch of 41 companies and found Alta 5 to have the third highest [Mattermark Growth Score](https://mattermark.com/top-25-500-startups-batch-20-demo-day-startups/) of the batch.

Miami’s [Court Buddy](http://www.miamiherald.com/news/business/article131507079.html), a legal-tech startup also part of 500 Startups’ Batch 20 that pitched Thursday (and a former Miami Herald Business Plan Challenge winner), made the top 25 in Mattermark’s ranking.
