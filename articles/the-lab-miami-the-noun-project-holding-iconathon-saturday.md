# The LAB Miami, The Noun Project holding “Iconathon” Saturday

[The LAB Miami](http://www.thelabmiami.com/) has teamed up with [The Noun Project](http://www.thenounproject.com/) to host an [Iconathon public design workshop](http://civichackersiconathon.eventbrite.com/) on Saturday, Feb. 16. According to the press release, participants of the hackathon-for-design workshop will create new visual tools to be used in civic hacking. The Iconathon is sponsored by the [John S. and James L. Knight Foundation](http://www.knightfoundation.org/) as part of its efforts to bring civic innovation to Miami.

The Iconathon will bring together designers, hackers, students and civic-minded Miami residents for a collaborative workshop to design symbols for public interest, the organizations said. The Noun Project is working with [Code for America’s Brigade](http://brigade.codeforamerica.org/) to identify which symbols are frequently needed by civic hackers when developing new civic apps and websites. The Brigade is an organizing force for local civic engagement -- a national network of “civic hackers” who contribute their skills and time to better their communities by way of technology. The final symbols will be donated to the public domain and available for anyone to download for free at [www.thenounproject.com](http://www.thenounproject.com/).

The workshop will be held in The LAB Miami’s newly opened collaborative work-learn space in the Wynwood Art District. “We are excited to explore the intersection between art, technology and civic engagement,” said Wifredo Fernandez, co-founder of The LAB Miami.

No coding or design skills are required to participate. Previous Iconathons have created public domain symbols for concepts like “human rights”, “food bank”, “electric car”, and “sustainable energy,” the organizations said.
