# View: What I love about Mayor Levine’s tech-hub comments? Our response

**By Norberto ‘Tito” Gil**

[![Norberto Gil Headshot - Copy](https://web.archive.org/web/20140305133007im_/http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e201a51162549e970c-300wi "Norberto Gil Headshot - Copy")](http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e201a51162549e970c-pi)

When I first read the[comments by Miami Beach Mayor Phil Levine,](http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/wonkblog/wp/2014/01/22/miami-beach-mayor-take-your-tech-start-up-gospel-and-shove-it/) I smiled. I smiled because I knew exactly what was going to happen: that this community would stand united in its mission to make Miami a major tech-hub.

See, Miami Beach is a major competitive advantage for us, but the soul of Miami is in its ***people***. We are a tough bunch that knows how to build something from nothing. And that’s what we’ve done over the last two-or-so years with the tech community (most of the assets registered on [MapYourStartup.co](http://www.mapyourstartup.co/) were founded less than two years ago). We’re in a city that’s ruled by traditional business – understandably so, with so many competitive advantages – and one startup at a time, one [LAB Miami](http://thelabrats.tumblr.com/) and [Refresh Miami](http://www.refreshmiami.com/) meet-up at a time, one Knight Foundation and Miami DDA investment at a time, one [Miami Open Coffee Club](https://www.facebook.com/groups/591898790837450/) meet-up at time, we’ve built something very real and amazing.

And in my opinion, the strength of what we’re building has perhaps never been more evident than this last week. After Mayor Levine’s comments, entrepreneurs and community leaders [Brian Breslin](view-miami-is-the-brand-miami-is-the-region.md), [Brian Brackeen](http://wlrn.org/post/tech-ceo-phillip-levine-miami-and-miami-beach-are-one), [Will Weinraub](an-open-letter-to-the-mayor-of-miami-beach-youre-wrong-and-heres-why.md), [Matt Haggman](view-a-new-miami.md), [Nancy Dahlberg](index.md), [Binsen Gonzalez,](can-miami-be-one.md) and several others made public comments in defense of our community as a whole. And I knew they would, *that* is why I am smiling. I knew that when the dust settled, we’d all walk around that much more united in our collective effort.
