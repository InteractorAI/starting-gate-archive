# Zuke Music was $10K winner at Babson WIN Lab finale event

![Zuke](https://web.archive.org/web/20170517234546im_/http://miamiherald.typepad.com/.a/6a00d83451b26169e201b7c8f637a9970b-800wi "Zuke")

Babson College’s  [Women Innovating Now (WIN) Lab](http://www.babson.edu/Academics/centers/cwel/educational-programs/win-lab/Pages/home.aspx) celebrated its inaugural cohort’s completion of the eight-month Miami program on April 25, with over 250 guests at The Jewel Box at the National YoungArts Foundation headquarters. The WIN Lab® expanded into Miami in 2016 with the support of  [The John S. and James L. Knight Foundation](http://www.babson.edu/news-events/babson-news/Pages/2016-babson-win-lab-launches-in-miami.aspx) and corporate sponsors including Akerman LLP.

WIN Lab Miami’s Director  [Carolina Pina](http://www.babson.edu/news-events/babson-news/Pages/2017-babson-names-carolina-pina-director-women-innovating-now-lab-miami.aspx) led the evening’s progression stating, “We are determined to impact the existing growth gap in the city; Miami ranks No. 2 in entrepreneurial activity but No. 38 in growth. By having more scalable businesses run and led by women, we’re hoping investors will be more willing to invest in them so we can also contribute to narrowing the funding gap.”

Matt Haggman, Knight Foundation Miami Program Director, added, “We’re trying to build a community that’s unlike any other. Innovation is built on a diversity of ideas and people.”

Haggman was one of three representatives judging WIN Lab Miami’s grand prize pitch session, with $17,500 in prizes sponsored by FedEx. For the pitch, three cohort companies who exceptionally leveraged the venture accelerator’s resources were given five minutes each to share their business vision in hopes of winning the grand prize.

Arielle Cohen’s Zuke Music—a digital networking platform for music industry professionals—took home the evening’s top honor of $10,000. (pictured above)

Kelly Pierce’s PIERCE Plan—a NCAA standards tracking software program—landed second place and $5,000.

Jessica Do’s Palmpress Coffee—a personal craft coffee maker for hot coffee and cold brew—earned third place and $2,500.

The accelerator is currently  [accepting applications](http://www.babson.edu/news-events/babson-news/Pages/2017-win-lab-miami-finale-women-entrepreneurship-pitch-competition.aspx) for its second cohort, which has been extended until May 19. WIN LAB Miami applications can be found at [www.thewinlab.org/apply](http://www.thewinlab.org/apply).
